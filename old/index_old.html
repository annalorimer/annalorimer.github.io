How did I get started in Software Engineering?

Early on I figured out that if I treated whatever I was doing as an experiment, I got support at home and left alone enough to test stuff out and try a lot of new things. This led to some really weird food combinations and strange things that I made out of paper and whatever else I could find. I pretty much l iked to mess around with anything I could get my hands on. This included the top-notch (at the time) Dell PC my parents bought when I was 9. So like in many families I became the go-to person for all things to do with the computer, especially since I was the only kid. One summer I did attend an HTML/CSS coding camp for girls at the local university and I enjoyed it and working with the other girls, but there weren’t really other opportunities around then to follow up afterwards. So I did lots of other things. Writing (in English & French), storyboards, film making, baking and training horses. What I really enjoyed about all these things were the different ways of thinking and problem solving that were involved with these activities. In the case of the large animals (horses) I had to give quite a bit of thought to thinking about things from their perspective, not just my own. I didn’t think of of this as “pre-engineering or CS” but the components and passion for problem solving were there in these activities. Teaching a 1500-pound horse to stand on a pedestal like a circus elephant takes a surprising amount of problem solving and critical thinking skills, not to mention perseverance.
